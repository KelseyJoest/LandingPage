Our Axolotls have the shiniest iridophores, which is when the melanophores cluster together in bands and/or spots in the skin, forming the spotted and banded pattern of the wild-type axolotls. Without iridophores to limit their migration, melanocytes spread all over the place, making the animal have a more uniform color, with few or no spots
